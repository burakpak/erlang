Erlang C — Call center calculator

Staffing & service level model for telecom customer service queues

Input parameters

Call arrival rate (λ) 100 calls/hr
Calls reaching your centre per hour. Use your peak hour, not daily average — that's where staffing decisions bite. Typical call centres: 50–300/hr.
Range: 10 – 500 calls/hr
Average handle time (AHT) 240 s (4:00)
Talk time + hold + after-call work (ACW) per call. Telecom support: typically 3–8 min. Billing & porting queries run longer than tech triage.
Range: 30 – 900 s
Active agents (N) 12 agents
Agents simultaneously logged in and available. Excludes supervisors, agents on break, or in training. Must exceed traffic load (Erlangs) to keep the queue finite.
Range: 1 – 80 agents
Service level target time (T) 20 s
The "T" in "X% of calls answered within T seconds". Telecom industry norm: 20 s (the 80/20 rule inherited from a 1983 AT&T study). Some regulators specify 30 s.
Range: 5 – 120 s

Model: Erlang C (M/M/N queue) — assumes Poisson arrivals, exponential service times, infinite patience (no abandonment). Real SL is typically higher than predicted because impatient callers self-remove from queue.  |  Formula: C(N,A) = [AN/N! · N/(N−A)] / [Σk=0..N−1 Ak/k! + AN/N! · N/(N−A)]  |  SL(T) = 1 − C(N,A) · e−(N−A)·T/AHT  |  ASA = C(N,A) · AHT / (N−A)
